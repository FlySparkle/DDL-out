# Community Code of Conduct

Last updated: 2026-07-15

The Chinese version is authoritative; this translation is provided for convenience. This code applies to the DDL out! repository, Issues, Discussions, code reviews, and other public spaces where someone represents the project.

## Our commitment

We are committed to an open, welcoming, safe, and harassment-free community. Every sincere participant should be treated with respect regardless of age, body, disability, ethnicity, gender identity or expression, experience, education, nationality, race, religion, sexual orientation, or socioeconomic status.

## Encouraged behaviour

- Participate with good faith, honesty, and empathy.
- Respect different perspectives, experiences, languages, and boundaries.
- Give and receive specific, constructive feedback.
- Acknowledge mistakes, take responsibility for impact, and repair harm where possible.
- Protect the long-term wellbeing of the community and project.

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, stalking, threats, intimidation, or personal attacks.
- Discrimination, derogatory remarks, or stereotypes based on identity or personal characteristics.
- Unwelcome sexualised language, imagery, or attention.
- Publishing another person's private or identifying information without permission.
- Impersonation, false attribution, or deliberate deception of the community.
- Spam, disruption, malicious distribution, or other conduct that threatens community safety.

## Reporting and privacy

Public discussion may be started through [GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/FlySparkle/DDL-out/discussions). This is a public channel: do not submit identity details, contact information, private records, or other sensitive content. Security vulnerabilities must use [GitHub private vulnerability reporting](https://github.com/FlySparkle/DDL-out/security/advisories/new) and must not be disclosed publicly.

The project currently offers no separate private channel for community conduct reports. If an event cannot be described publicly without exposing sensitive information, do not publish those details.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove content, issue warnings, restrict participation temporarily, or impose a permanent ban. The response should reflect severity, repetition, impact, and willingness to repair harm. Maintainers should avoid spreading unnecessary personal information and remain accountable for enforcement decisions.
